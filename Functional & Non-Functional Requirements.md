# Functional Requirements:
- Concerned with **WHAT** the system *must* achieve
	- Example:
	- The rocket must bring back to earth the crew inside alive
	- The rocket must reach the moon
#### What constitutes a functional requirement?
Functional requirements describe the specific:
- functions
- inputs
- outputs
- calculations
- data manipulation
- and business rules 
that the system must meet
# Non-Functional Requirements:
- Concerned with **HOW** "*well*"the system must achieve it's requirements:
	- Example:
	- The rocket must get to the moon in 72 hrs or less
	- The rocket must be operable within 1 month of specified training

#### What constitutes a non-functional requirement?
- Performance requirements (response time, throughput, scalability)
- Usability requirements (user interface, accessibility, ease of use)
- Reliability and availability requirements (fault tolerance, recovery, backup)
- Security requirements (authentication, authorization, data encryption)
- Maintainability requirements (modularity, extensibility, testability)
- Compatibility requirements (cross-platform, browser compatibility)
- Environmental requirements (hardware, software, network)
