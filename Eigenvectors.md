# What is:
##  Need to solve: $(A -\lambda I)\hat v = 0$
#### $A \hat v = \lambda \hat v$
- $\hat v$ is the eigen**vector**
- $\lambda$ is the [[Eigenvalue]]

# Strategy/Motivation:
- Sometimes $A \hat v = \lambda \hat v$ where $\hat v$ is a vector, is true
	- $\therefore$ $A$ simply scales $\hat v$
- We want to find particular vectors which are only **scaled** by A & then **transform** A into a new [[Basis]] defined by these vectors

# Notes:
- Corresponding Matrix after applying lambda scaling at then I matrix **SHOULD NOT** be [[Linear Independence]] 
	- $\therefore$ Equation gathered will have no solid solution (usually just pick x=1)
	- Hence, eigenvectors only show **DIRECTION, NOT LENGTH**
