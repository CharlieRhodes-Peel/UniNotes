- Subsets are NOT elements, they HAVE to be sets
# Notation:
## $X \subset Y$ where $X$ is a subset of $Y$:
- means: $x \in X$ & $x \in Y$
- but also where: $y \in Y$ & $y \notin X$
### $X \subseteq Y$ where X is a subset of Y:
- means: $x \in X$ & $x \in Y$ & $X = Y$
## $X \subseteq X$ & $\varnothing \subseteq X$ are always true

# Example:
## A = {2,4,6}, B = {1,2,3,4,5,6}
### A $\subseteq$ B, "A is a subset OF B" 

## Proving:
- In order to prove that two sets are equal you should prove that both sides are subset of each other, if they are then they are equal
