[[Requirement Gathering]] + [[Analysis]] Feedback on each other!:
- "[[Requirement Gathering]] & [[Analysis]] happen concurrently & iteratively"
- Requirement gathering is for discussion between client & developers
- Errors introduced in the requirement stage are expensive to fix so it is good to test and get feedback from end users about the proposed solution

# Definition and Scope:
There are two main philosophies, traditionally scope is fixed. But [[agile]] accepts changes and accepts that scope may change over time and embraces it.
But both roughly follow these steps: 
- Need: what problem it solves?
	- "Earth is not sustainable and so humans will have to move to other planets"
- [[Software Goals]]: what's intended purpose / functionality?
	- "Use a rocket to fly humans to other planets within a reasonable time frame"
- Business: how will make money?
	- "Can let public agencies benefits from our research (for a price) and charge customers money"
- Stakeholders: **ANY** person who will be affected by system
	- "Elon musk, public agencies, customers, etc..."
- Overview: High level view of how system will be used (by user)
	- i can't be asked to keep going
- Success Criteria: How can success be measured
	- "24 rocket launches by 2026"

# Example of Process:
Using the MySouthampton App as an example
Need: Provide information to student about their course and campus
Goals: To show student's their timetables and a map of campus
Business: Helps students get more comfortable with uni life, less chance to drop out
Stakeholders: Students, Lectures, Uni administration, any investors
1 example success criteria: A student must have their correct timetable displayed for each week of a semester.

# What makes a good specification:
- Consistency - No contradictions
- Unambiguous - Doesn't lead to contradictions (misinterpretations)
- Verifiability - Correctness cab be **repeatably** tested
- Traceability - Requirements can be mapped to functionalities in the system. 


# Common Errors:
- Users don't always know what they want
	- This can lead to conflicting requirements
	- Often need help to innovate
	- May not have an idea of the full picture - & $\therefore$ are constrained to the limits of what they can imagine
