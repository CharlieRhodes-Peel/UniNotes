Imagine a market with "cherries" and "lemons" where cherries are considered high-quality and lemons are considered low-quality. Now imagine that a seller sells both lemon and cherries; however, they package them both in the same packaging so the buyer doesn't know if they are getting a cherry or a lemon

#### How the market will react to this?
Most people will pay an "average" price for these. However, this is bad for everyone as eventually, Cherry sellers will stop selling to this buyer as they can get higher prices else where.
- Results in only lemons left as they can sell them for an increased price!
- This will not last in the long-term however as people will not pay for over-priced lemons!

## Leads to market failure!

This is an example of an **asymmetric** transaction, where the buyer knows LESS than the seller. This is BAD for the overall market :0
