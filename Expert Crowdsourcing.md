This will look at answering the question, how do you crowdsource experts? Well first lets say how it differs from traditional [[Human Computation]] problems. With expert crowdsourcing, you need specialists / experts in an area with specific knowledge. The **quality** of the work is key, and often completing tasks can be very costly for the workers. Requesters can often select workers instead of the other way around.

# In the Context of Contests
The general idea is that the requester describes a problem and offers a reward. They then pour over submissions and **choose** the worker with the best solution to give the reward to. There can be multiple rounds, prizes and iterative feedback given.

It's a popular approach for finding expert workers; however, can be inefficient.
- Many workers provide services without getting paid or having their work utilised
- Workers need to strategise - so the risk of losing can be disincentivising

The alternative is just to hire a small group of workers for a specific task (like freelance work)
# Taskcn.com
Taskcn is one other these expert crowdsourcing sites. And looking at the data only ~3.2% of users actually received a reward. Because of this, users who win on their first task are way more likely to continue contributing. A **small proportion** of users do very well and improve over time. ~**0.1%** of active users won **19.9%** of contests!
### Implications
- Workers behave strategically
- Early experience is critical
- Small population of highly productive / successful workers
