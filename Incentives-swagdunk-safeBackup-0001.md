# Summary
- Intrinsic motivation can be a powerful incentive
- Introducing payments affects behaviour - which can be **positive** or **negative**
- On [[Mechanical Turk]], evidence suggests that rising *magnitude* of payments lead to higher output but not higher quality
- Structure of payment can affect quality - Per-task, per-annotation, per-time
- Not all workers are greedy - many are concerned about the quality of their results

# Manipulating Incentives
We can manipulate incentives to make them "better" for example by providing more money in the financial case, or perhaps gamifying the experience more to make it more enjoyable, all of the 5 aspects are exploitable in some way.

So the more incentives we offer, the more successful our crowdsourcing project, right? Well actually no. There's a relationship between these different incentives.
- Financial rewards **decrease** intrinsic motivation
- Positive verbal feedback **increases** intrinsic motivation

# Non-Monetary Incentives
**Gamifying** the process. One example of this is **Output-Agreement** it's basically the minigame of "What do you see" and you need to produce outputs until there is a match. With discovered labels added as taboo words.

**Gamifying** the process involves adding things the user, "player" can achieve. Like points, badges, medals, leaderboards, progress bars.

## Referral Incentives
Another good way to add Non-Monetary Incentives is though **Non-Monetary Referral Incentives**, an example of one of these would be a **collective goal** to reach x **points** for cancer research say. BUT! **Referring** **a** **friend** to do the same will also add a **point** to the total, For **free**! This is good for the **user** because they **feel** like they are doing **something good** as the points go up. But also for the charity as they spend less money on **advertising**!

### Case study
Out of 4 different groups ("treatments") Between 1 point per referral, 3 points for referral, no points for referral (control) and a recursive option where 1 point per referral, 1/2 point for indirect referral and so on...

The 3-point group resulted in the most overall referrals!
